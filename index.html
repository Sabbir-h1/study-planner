17 + 14 chapters
Do minimum 5 board-style problems per chapter every single day — no exceptions. Start easier chapters first to build confidence, then tackle the hard ones. Every wrong answer goes into your mistake diary and gets redone next session until correct. Never skip Math, even for 1 day.

Physics
13 chapters
Make a single one-page formula sheet and read it every morning. Solve at least 3 numericals per session. Draw all circuit/ray diagrams yourself — don't just look at them. Physics MCQs are very pattern-based; collect last 7 years of SSC board MCQs and drill them repeatedly.

12 chapters
Make flashcards for every chemical equation and reaction. Understand why reactions happen — don't just memorize. Chemistry is one of the highest-scoring MCQ subjects if you nail the patterns. Past board MCQs repeat almost identically year to year.

Habits that make A+ students
📓 The 3-notebook system
Keep three notebooks. Notebook 1: main notes while learning. Notebook 2: one-page formula and key-point sheet per subject — read this every morning. Notebook 3: mistake diary — every wrong MCQ and every mistake gets written here, then redone until correct. The mistake diary is where A+ students are made.
⏱️ 45-minute focus blocks
Study for exactly 45 minutes, then take a 10-minute break — stand up, walk, drink water. Repeat. Never sit for more than 2 hours straight. Your brain stops absorbing after that. Memory consolidates during rest, not during study.
📋 Weekly self-test every Sunday
Every Sunday, close your notes and test yourself on the week's material for 1 hour. No peeking. This forces real recall and reveals exactly what you don't know. This one habit separates A+ students from everyone else — the brain only truly learns what it is forced to retrieve.
🎯 Board question obsession
Collect the last 7 years of SSC Dhaka board questions for every subject. In Bangladeshi boards, 60–70% of MCQs repeat in patterns year after year. For math and science, almost all question structures repeat. Working through these is not shortcutting — it's the smartest thing you can do.
💤 Sleep = free extra marks
Sleep 7.5–8 hours every single night. Your brain moves short-term memory into permanent storage during deep sleep. Students who cut sleep to study more actually remember significantly less the next day. Bed by 9:30pm, wake at 6am. This is not optional — it is a study technique.
📵 Phone in another room
During every study session, put your phone in a different room — not face down, not silent, in another room. Each notification breaks 20 minutes of deep focus. 43 days of phone discipline equals A+ for life. Use phone only during scheduled 10-minute breaks.
🍽️ Eat and drink properly
Your brain uses 20% of your body's energy. Eat proper meals — don't skip lunch. Drink water constantly while studying. Dehydration of even 1–2% measurably reduces concentration and memory. Keep a water bottle on your desk at all times.
A+ doesn't come from studying the most hours. It comes from studying the right things, the right way, consistently. Follow this plan and you will get there. You can do this. 🏆
